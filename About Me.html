My Education Journey


In this series, you'll get to know a little about me and my journey. I am currently a student at the University of Technology, Jamaica, pursuing a degree in Computer Science. It's been a long road to get where I am today, and I still have two more years before completing my studies.

When I graduated from high school ten years ago, I was unsure of what career path to take. My brother encouraged me to apply to UTECH for Midwifery, but I didn’t have the necessary qualifications and wasn’t accepted. Determined to move forward, I went back to school to retake CXC Mathematics and Human and Social Biology. Even then, I still couldn’t decide on a career, so I started applying for various jobs. As the years passed, I felt a growing desire to pursue a career, but I struggled to find something that truly matched my passion and personality. At one point, I applied to Shortwood Teachers College, thinking I could follow in my sister’s footsteps and become a teacher. While waiting for acceptance, I discovered I was two months pregnant, and my life took an unexpected turn. Regret and uncertainty filled my mind as everything seemed to spiral out of control.

A year and a half after giving birth, I decided to study Food and Beverage at an HEART/TRUST institution. During that year, I realized that my true passion wasn’t in the hospitality industry. After completing my studies and work experience, I took time to reflect on all the jobs I had held and what aspects I enjoyed most. It all became clear—while working at a graphics store, I had been fascinated by computers, always curious about how they function and how they are programmed. With that realization, I reapplied to UTECH, but this time for a BSc Degree in Computing, and I’ve been on that path ever since.
